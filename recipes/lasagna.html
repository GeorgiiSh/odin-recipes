Lasagna

Home

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian‑inspired baked pasta dish made by layering wide, flat noodles with a rich meat (or vegetable) sauce, creamy cheese filling, and melting mozzarella. It emerges from the oven bubbling and golden‑brown on top, with tender pasta sheets soaked in savory tomato sauce and pockets of creamy, gooey cheese running through every forkful. Here’s a straightforward, “every‑day” lasagna you can customize to your taste

Ingredients

  • 12–15 lasagna noodles (regular or no‑boil)
  • 1 lb (450 g) ground beef (or half beef/half pork; or swap for mushrooms or lentils)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2–3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 (28 oz/800 g) can crushed tomatoes
  • 2 (6 oz/170 g) cans tomato paste (or 1 small can)
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • Pinch of red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 15 oz (425 g) ricotta cheese
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan (plus extra for topping)
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella (plus extra for topping)
  • 2 Tbsp chopped fresh parsley or basil (optional garnish)
  • Olive oil, for sautéing

Steps

  1. Sauce: Sauté onion and garlic, brown the meat, then stir in tomatoes, herbs, salt & pepper; simmer 15 min.
  2. Cheese mix: Stir ricotta, egg, Parmesan, and herbs until smooth.
  3. Assemble: Layer sauce, noodles, ricotta, and mozzarella in a 9×13" dish (repeat twice), finishing with cheese.
  4. Bake: Covered at 375 °F (190 °C) for 25 min, uncover and bake 15 min more; rest 10 min before serving.
